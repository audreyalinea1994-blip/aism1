Internal Control Framework

Definition: Internal controls are policies, procedures, and mechanisms designed to safeguard assets, ensure accurate financial reporting, promote operational efficiency, and ensure compliance with regulations.

COSO Framework Components

The Committee of Sponsoring Organizations (COSO) framework is the gold standard for internal control design.

1. Control Environment

The foundation - includes integrity, ethical values, and competence of personnel. Sets the "tone at the top."

2. Risk Assessment

Identifying and analyzing risks that could prevent achieving objectives. Includes fraud risk assessment.

3. Control Activities

Actions taken to address risks - authorizations, approvals, verifications, reconciliations, and segregation of duties.

4. Information & Communication

Relevant information must be captured and communicated in a timely manner to enable people to carry out their responsibilities.

5. Monitoring Activities

Ongoing evaluations, separate evaluations, or a combination to ascertain whether controls are present and functioning.

Risk Assessment in AIS

Risk assessment involves identifying potential threats to the AIS and evaluating their likelihood and impact. This process helps organizations prioritize control implementation.

Risk Assessment Process

🔍

1. Identify Risks

  • • Data breaches
  • • Unauthorized access
  • • System failures
  • • Human errors
📊

2. Analyze Risks

  • • Likelihood assessment
  • • Impact evaluation
  • • Vulnerability analysis
  • • Threat modeling
⚖️

3. Evaluate Risks

  • • Risk prioritization
  • • Cost-benefit analysis
  • • Risk tolerance levels
  • • Residual risk acceptance
🛡️

4. Treat Risks

  • • Avoid the risk
  • • Transfer the risk
  • • Mitigate the risk
  • • Accept the risk

💡 Risk Matrix

Organizations often use a risk matrix plotting Likelihood (x-axis) against Impact (y-axis) to visualize and prioritize risks. High likelihood + High impact = Critical priority.

IT Controls in AIS

IT controls are specific controls related to the technology infrastructure supporting the AIS. They are divided into two main categories:

🌐 General Controls (ITGCs)

Apply across all applications and IT infrastructure

Access Controls

User authentication, authorization levels, password policies

Change Management

System updates, testing procedures, approval workflows

Operations Controls

Backup procedures, disaster recovery, job scheduling

Physical Security

Data center access, environmental controls

⚙️ Application Controls

Specific to individual applications/processes

Input Controls

Data validation, format checks, completeness verification

Processing Controls

Batch totals, run-to-run totals, reasonableness tests

Output Controls

Report distribution, reconciliation, review procedures

Audit Trails

Transaction logging, user activity tracking

🔑 Key IT Control Concepts

Segregation of Duties

No single person controls all aspects of a transaction

Least Privilege

Users get minimum access needed for their role

Defense in Depth

Multiple layers of security controls

Fraud Prevention

The Fraud Triangle

Developed by criminologist Donald Cressey, the fraud triangle explains why people commit fraud:

💰

Pressure

Financial need or incentive

🚪

Opportunity

Weak controls allow fraud

🧠

Rationalization

Justifying the behavior

Types of AIS Fraud

💳 Asset Misappropriation

  • • Skimming cash receipts
  • • Ghost employees
  • • Inventory theft
  • • Expense reimbursement fraud

📑 Financial Statement Fraud

  • • Revenue overstatement
  • • Expense understatement
  • • Improper asset valuation
  • • Concealed liabilities

Prevention Strategies

✓

Segregation of Duties

Separate authorization, custody, and recording functions

✓

Regular Reconciliations

Independent verification of records against physical assets

✓

Access Controls & Audit Trails

Limit system access and track all transactions

✓

Ethics & Training Programs

Foster ethical culture and awareness of fraud indicators

Individual Case Study Activity

Case: TechFlow Solutions Data Breach

Background: TechFlow Solutions is a mid-sized software company with 200 employees. They use an integrated AIS for all financial operations including accounts payable, accounts receivable, payroll, and general ledger.

The Incident: In March 2024, the company discovered that over ₱15 million had been siphoned from company accounts over 18 months. The investigation revealed the following:

  • A senior accountant, Maria Santos, had been creating fictitious vendor accounts and processing payments to herself
  • Maria had access to create vendors, approve invoices, AND process payments
  • The company had no systematic review of new vendor additions
  • Password for the AIS admin account was shared among three staff members
  • Audit logs were enabled but never reviewed
  • Bank reconciliations were performed by the same person who processed payments
  • The company had recently laid off staff, and Maria had complained about being underpaid

Discovery: The fraud was only discovered when Maria went on extended sick leave and her temporary replacement noticed unusual vendor payment patterns.
